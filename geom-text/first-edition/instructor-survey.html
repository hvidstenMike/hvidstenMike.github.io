Geometry Project Field-Test Survey


Goals and Objectives:

The goals for the author in writing the textbook, and in producing the accompanying software, were

a) to employ a discovery-based learning approach as an integral component in the teaching and learning of college geometry
b) to integrate technology into the geometry course by developing robust geometry software to accompany the written course materials
c) to facilitate change in the instruction of college geometry by providing faculty with a rich set of resources with which to implement the previous goals
d) to affect the attitudes of future secondary math teachers toward discovery-learning and the use of technology with the goal of having students implement these strategies in their own teaching.

1. Based on your experience in teaching geometry from the project materials, how effective has the project been in meeting the first goal, the integration of discovery-learning into college geometry? 








2. How well has the project met the second goal, the integration of technology?









3. Have the project materials been helpful to you in designing and teaching college geometry with a focus on discovery-learning and technology? (the third goal)









4. Please give your overall assessment in how effective the project materials have been in shaping student attitudes toward the first two goals.   For those students who are planning careers in education, have the project materials been effective in giving them a model for their own teaching?










Content:

7.  Which sections did you cover? Please comment on parts of the text that you found essential, and also on parts that could be omitted.

11. Would it be helpful to have sample exams and alternate projects in the instructor's guide?
